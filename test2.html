National Cadet Corps (NCC)

The National Cadet Corps (NCC) is the youth wing of the Indian Armed Forces, headquartered in New Delhi. It is a voluntary organization open to school and college students across India, operating as a Tri-Services organization that includes the Army, Navy, and Air Force wings. The NCC recruits cadets from educational institutions nationwide, providing them with basic military training in small arms, physical fitness and parade drills.

Beyond military training, the NCC emphasises personality development, social responsibility and community service, aiming to nurture disciplined, patriotic, responsible and united citizens.

Motto: “Unity and Discipline (एकता और अनुशासन)”, adopted on 23rd December 1957, reflects NCC’s aim to unite youth from across the country and instill discipline and national integration.

The TCET-NCC Unit was established with a sanctioned strength of 104 Army Wing Cadets & 50 Naval Wing Cadets on 14th October 2021 and 27th October 2023 respectively. The unit conducts regular training and activities under the guidance of:
Cdr. Vijay Pratap Singh (Mentor & Officer In-charge), Lt. (Dr.) Nivant Kambale (Associate NCC Officer, Army Wing), Mr. Sunil Khatri (Care Taker Officer, Navy Wing).

The TCET-NCC Unit is committed to developing disciplined, responsible and united citizens in line with the NCC motto.
