National Cadet Corps (NCC)

The National Cadet Corps (NCC) is the youth wing of the Indian Armed Forces, headquartered in New Delhi. It is a voluntary organization open to school and college students across India, operating as a Tri-Services organization that includes the Army, Navy, and Air Force wings.

The NCC recruits cadets from educational institutions nationwide, providing them with basic military training in small arms, physical fitness and parade drills. Beyond military training, the NCC emphasizes personality development, social responsibility, and community service.

Motto: “Unity and Discipline (एकता और अनुशासन)” – Adopted on 23rd December 1957, it reflects the aim to unite youth from across the country and instill a sense of discipline and national integration.

The TCET-NCC Unit was established with a sanctioned strength of 104 Army Wing Cadets on 14th October 2021 and 50 Naval Wing Cadets on 27th October 2023. The unit conducts regular training and activities under the able guidance of:

  • Cdr. Vijay Pratap Singh – Mentor & Officer In-charge
  • Lt. (Dr.) Nivant Kambale – Associate NCC Officer, Army Wing
  • Mr. Sunil Khatri – Care Taker Officer, Navy Wing

The TCET NCC Unit is committed to shaping disciplined, service-minded and nationally integrated youth in line with the NCC's guiding principles.

Key Features

  • First-of-its-kind: Pioneer among private engineering colleges in Maharashtra with official NCC sanction under the Ministry of Defence.
  • 'C' Certificate Advantage: NCC cadets who successfully earn the ‘C’ Certificate are exempted from the written exam in various Indian Armed Forces recruitment processes.
  • Prestigious Parade Representation: Outstanding cadets are nominated for Republic Day and Independence Day parades in Mumbai and New Delhi.
  • Global Exposure: Selected cadets are deputed for international deployments, training programs, and the Youth Exchange Programme (YEP).
  • Combat Readiness: Hands-on training in small arms and weapon handling conducted by qualified Army personnel.
  • Holistic Learning: Sessions on national integration, safety, security, and health to instill social awareness and civic responsibility.
  • Career Mentorship: Periodic motivational lectures and expert guidance on “How to Join the Indian Armed Forces.”

Our Objectives

The TCET NCC Unit aims to nurture students into responsible citizens and future leaders, equipped with strength of character and a spirit of service.

  • Leadership Development: Create a pool of well-trained, motivated youth who can take up leadership roles across diverse sectors, including defense services.
  • Character Building: Foster qualities such as discipline, camaraderie, integrity, and selfless service among cadets.
  • Community Engagement: Conduct socially impactful activities to contribute towards community development and national integration.
  • Career Facilitation: Provide a structured environment that inspires engineering students to pursue a career in the Indian Armed Forces.
  • Adventure & Resilience: Encourage a spirit of adventure, resilience, and secularism through exposure to real-life challenges and collaborative missions.
"At TCET NCC, we don’t just train cadets—we inspire a generation committed to serve the nation with honor and pride."
